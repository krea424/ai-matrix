# Product Guidelines: AI Matrix - Design & Interaction Principles

## 1. Visual Identity: Modern Dark Strategy
*   **Color Palette:**
    *   **Backgrounds:** Deep Obsidian (#0B0E14), Charcoal Grey (#1A1D23).
    *   **Accents (The "Glowing" Layer):** Electric Cyan (#00F5FF) for high feasibility, Amethyst Violet (#BF40BF) for high impact, and Slate Grey for low-priority items.
    *   **Typography:** High-readability sans-serif (e.g., Inter or Geist) for data. Sophisticated, wide-track headers for a premium feel.
*   **Aesthetic Goal:** To create a "Cinematic Cockpit" that feels like a high-end financial terminal or a strategic war room.

## 2. Interaction Model: Progressive Revelation
*   **The Narrative Stepper:** The assessment is divided into logical phases (e.g., *Strategic Alignment*, *Technical Readiness*, *Economic Impact*).
*   **Dynamic Feedback:** As the consultant moves sliders or selects options, subtle visual cues (progress bars or pulsing data points) indicate the immediate effect on the overall score.
*   **Focus State:** Non-active sections are dimmed to guide the consultant and client through the specific variable under discussion.

## 3. Data Entry: Smart & Defensive
*   **Contextual Sidebars:** Every variable input is paired with an optional "Strategic Rationale" field. This allows the consultant to document the "why" without cluttering the main interface.
*   **Input Precision:** Use custom-designed sliders and "smart" buttons that snap to qualitative labels (e.g., "Low", "Medium", "High") while storing precise quantitative values.

## 4. Executive Presentation Mode
*   **The "Reveal" Animation:** The final Matrix should animate use cases into position, creating a sense of discovery and analytical rigor.
*   **Interactive Tooltips:** During a C-level presentation, clicking a use case on the Matrix opens a "Quick Fact Sheet" pulling from the contextual sidebars.
*   **High-Contrast Export:** One-click generation of a "Executive Summary" PDF that maintains the dark aesthetic but is optimized for clarity and digital sharing.

## 5. Tone & Voice
*   **Professional, Authoritative, and Direct.**
*   The UI should use industry-standard terminology (e.g., "ROI Path", "Technical Debt", "Market Differentiation") to resonate with C-Suite audiences.
